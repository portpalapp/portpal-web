Streak System

Streaks leverage loss aversion, the most powerful behavioral hook. Once a worker has a 15-day streak, the psychological cost of breaking it drives daily logging even on off days (logging "no work" counts).

Streak Types

Leaderboard Design

Leaderboard Rules

  • ✓ Anonymous by default. Users identified by board position (e.g., "B-Man #47") and badge count. No real names unless user opts in.
  • ✓ Monthly reset. Fresh competition every month prevents runaway leaders from demoralizing newcomers. All-time stats shown separately.
  • ✓ Board position grouping. A-Board vs B-Board vs Casual leaderboards so casuals aren't competing against full-timers who work 5x more shifts.
  • ✓ Multiple categories. Shifts logged, streak length, badges earned, points total, pay stubs uploaded. Different people can lead different boards.
  • ✓ No earnings displayed. Never show pay amounts on leaderboards. Privacy-first. Compete on activity, not income.
  • ✓ Terminal leaderboards. "Top logger at Centennial this month" creates location-based competition and community.

Reverse Trial Model

Every new user gets full Pro for 30 days with no credit card required. This lets them experience the "aha moment" (catching a pay discrepancy, seeing earnings predictions) before the paywall appears.

Day 0: Sign Up

Full Pro access activated. Subtle "Pro Trial: 30 days remaining" badge in header. No friction, no CC.

Days 1-7: Onboarding Week

Guided to log shifts, upload first pay stub, explore analytics. First-week challenge (log 3 shifts) triggers badge. System learns their patterns.

Day 15: Mid-Trial Nudge

"15 days of Pro left. You've tracked $4,200 in earnings and found 1 rate discrepancy worth $23." Value-focused, not pressure.

Day 21: Value Summary

Full dashboard of what Pro has done: shifts logged, discrepancies caught, money insights generated. "Here's what you'll lose..."

Day 28: Soft Countdown

"2 days left. Lock in $99/year -- that's less than you make in the first 15 minutes of a day shift." Terminal-specific social proof.

Day 30: Paywall Activates

Pro features locked. Free features remain fully functional. Data is never deleted. Upgrade prompt on locked features shows their personal stats.

Day 30+: Win-Back

Next pay stub upload attempt shows: "Upgrade to compare this stub. Last time, we found $23 you were owed." Periodic re-trial offers (7-day) after 60 days.

Cultural Dynamics

  • ● Tight-knit community. Everyone knows everyone at the dispatch hall. Word travels fast, both good and bad.
  • ● Skeptical of outsiders. Initial trust barrier is high. But once in, loyalty is fierce. Need a "one of us" champion.
  • ● Competitive about earnings. Workers compare notes on best shifts, terminals, OT opportunities. They want to optimize.
  • ● Union solidarity. Any tool perceived as helping management or undermining the union will be rejected immediately.
  • ● Generational divide. Younger workers (25-35) are more tech-savvy and open to apps. Older workers need peer proof.
  • ● Shift talk culture. Workers discuss shifts, pay, and conditions constantly. PORTPAL data becomes conversation fuel.

Dispatch Hall Culture

Workers don't have fixed schedules. They go to the dispatch hall (physical building) and get assigned shifts based on seniority. This means:

  • ● Workers don't know their shift until dispatch (sometimes night before via text)
  • ● Casuals may sit in the hall for hours hoping to get dispatched
  • ● A-Board workers get first pick, B-Board second, Casuals last
  • ● Terminal and job assignment varies daily
  • ● Missing a dispatch call can mean losing a day's pay ($400-700)

Union Endorsement Strategy

A formal union endorsement is the single highest-leverage growth event. It transforms PORTPAL from "some app" to "the app the union recommends."

Phase 1: Demonstrate Value (Current)

957 users at Local 500 already proving value organically. Collect testimonials. Document pay discrepancies caught. Build the case with real data: "$41M tracked, X discrepancies found totaling $Y."

Phase 2: Approach Union Leadership

Request meeting with Local 500 executive. Present: "957 of your members already use this. Here's what it does for them." Offer union-endorsed pricing ($69/year). Emphasize: worker empowerment tool, not management tool.

Phase 3: Pilot Partnership

If receptive, propose pilot: union mentions PORTPAL in monthly newsletter or at next general meeting. PORTPAL provides free Pro to all Local 500 members for 90 days. Measure signup and retention.

Phase 4: Formal Endorsement

After successful pilot, negotiate formal endorsement. Union gets: free Pro for all members (subsidized by PORTPAL), anonymized workforce insights (aggregate only), input on feature roadmap. PORTPAL gets: official endorsement, dispatch hall placement, newsletter inclusion, introduction to other locals.

Phase 5: Cascade to Other Locals

Local 500 endorsement creates credibility for approaching Local 502 (New Westminster, 3,000+ workers), then 505, 508, 514, 517. Each endorsement is easier than the last. ILWU regional endorsement becomes possible.
