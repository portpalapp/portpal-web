1 Problem Statement

BC longshoremen track shifts manually in spreadsheets and notes apps. Pay errors are common — workers eat the losses because verifying 990+ rate combinations is too hard without tooling.

But the bigger problem: casual workers drive 1-2 hours to the dispatch hall every day with no guarantee of getting work. When jobs are slow, that's time and gas money — gone. There's no tool that tells them whether it's worth making the trip.

2 Demand Evidence

957 users logging shifts without being asked to pay. That's organic demand.

Pay errors are common, workers eat them. The status quo is spreadsheets and trust — nobody has the records to challenge discrepancies.

62.5% 90-day retention on a free app with no push notifications, no gamification loop, no AI features. Users come back because the core problem is real.

Magic number: Users who log 10+ shifts in month 1 retain at 69.9%. The product hooks when it becomes habit.

3 Approved Premises

✓
Free tier stays free forever. Shift tracking, rate calculations, and basic stats are the acquisition funnel, not the product.
✓
The paid product is an AI intelligence layer that helps workers make future decisions — not just record past shifts.
✓
Dispatch prediction is the single most valuable feature — it saves real time and money daily, not just at payday.
✓
Must analyze 73K existing shifts before building. There are user behavior patterns in that data that will tell you what to build and what to skip.

4 Approaches Considered

A: "Dispatch Oracle" — Narrowest Wedge

Effort: M Risk: Medium

Build dispatch prediction only. Scrape job board data, combine with user-reported outcomes, ship a daily "should I go?" notification. Single feature, single value prop.

B: "Intelligence Suite" — Full AI Layer

Effort: L Risk: Med-High

Build everything at once: dispatch predictions + seniority tracking + AI chat + pension forecasting. More value, but can't A/B test which feature converts.

C then A: "Data First, Then Oracle"

Effort: S+M Risk: Low

Mine the 73K shifts for behavioral insights first (2 days CC), then build the Dispatch Oracle with confidence that you're building the right thing. Evidence-based, de-risked, focused.
