Priority Improvements (Data-Driven)

1. Guided First-Week Onboarding

Impact: HIGH | Effort: MEDIUM

The single highest-leverage change you can make. The data shows:

  • 1 shift in week 1 = 59% churn. 3 shifts = 42%. 6 shifts = 32%.
  • If a user comes back after day 1, churn drops from 89% to 38%
  • Power users averaged 5 first-week shifts vs. 3.6 for churned users

What to build:

  • Day-1 welcome screen: "Log your first shift now" with pre-filled fields from their profile
  • Day-2 push notification: "How was yesterday's shift? Log it in 30 seconds"
  • Day-3: "You're on a 2-day streak. Most workers find their rhythm by day 5"
  • Day-5: "You've logged [X] shifts. Workers who log 6 in their first week stay with PORTPAL long-term"
  • Day-7: Celebration screen if 3+ shifts, rescue prompt if 0-1
Projected impact: Converting 30% of first-week churners (138 users) to 3+ shift users would reduce overall churn by ~5-8 percentage points. At scale with 7,200 BC workers, that's 360-576 additional retained users = $35K-$57K additional ARR.

2. Lapse Detection & Re-engagement

Impact: HIGH | Effort: MEDIUM

You currently have zero systems to detect and re-engage lapsing users. Build automated triggers:

  • 7-day lapse (user has 10+ shifts but hasn't logged in 7 days): Push notification — "Haven't seen you this week. Quick-log your shifts before you forget"
  • 14-day lapse: Email — "Your shift log is waiting. Here's what you might be missing in pay verification"
  • 21-day lapse: Personal-feeling message — "Workers who track regularly catch an average of $X in pay discrepancies"
  • 30-day lapse: Win-back offer for Pro users or "We saved your data" reassurance
Projected impact: Re-engaging 20% of the 66 long-term churners = 13 high-value users recovered. Preventing churn on the current 46 at-risk users at a 50% save rate = 23 more retained.

3. Empty-Job-Field Fix

Impact: MEDIUM | Effort: LOW

Users with no first job have 80% churn. This is a simple fix:

  • Make job selection mandatory on first shift entry (or during onboarding)
  • Pre-populate common jobs (LABOUR, TRACTOR TRAILER, RTG) as easy tap targets
  • Add "I don't know yet" option that follows up later rather than leaving blank

4. Smart Shift Entry (Reduce Friction)

Impact: MEDIUM | Effort: MEDIUM

Active users log 4 shifts/week. That's 4x they open the app and fill in fields. Reduce this friction:

  • "Same as last shift" button — pre-fill job, location, shift type from previous entry
  • Shift templates — "My usual day shift at Vanterm" saves as a one-tap entry
  • Batch entry — "Log the whole week" screen for users who forgot and want to catch up
  • Smart defaults — if user always works TRACTOR TRAILER at CENTENNIAL, default to that
Key insight: The churned users who lasted 6+ months had 3.55 shifts/active_week vs. 4.0 for active users. The difference is tiny — they may have churned not from disinterest but from friction fatigue. Anything that saves even 10 seconds per entry compounds over hundreds of shifts.

5. Pay Discrepancy Alerts (Value Demonstration)

Impact: HIGH | Effort: HIGH

The app's core value prop is catching pay errors. But users currently have to discover discrepancies themselves. Make the app actively surface them:

  • After a shift is logged: "Based on your job, shift, and location, your expected pay is $X. Does your stub match?"
  • Weekly digest: "You worked 4 shifts this week for estimated $X total. Verify when your stub arrives."
  • Discrepancy detection: flag when a user's logged rate differs from the standard rate for that job/shift
This turns passive logging into active money-finding. Nothing retains a user like the app literally showing them they were underpaid $47.50.

6. Target New Casuals (TRAINING First-Job Signal)

Impact: MEDIUM | Effort: LOW

Users who start with TRAINING as their first job have 19% churn — the lowest of any job category. These are new/casual workers who need to track hours for seniority. They are your ideal user:

  • Market directly at the union hall dispatch line — that's where casuals go every morning
  • Messaging: "Track your hours for seniority. Never lose a qualifying day."
  • Onboarding flow: "Are you casual or full-time?" — different experiences for each
  • Seniority tracker feature: days worked toward next seniority level

Challenge & Streak System

Gamification works when the mechanics align with real user goals. Longshoremen aren't playing a game — they're tracking money and seniority. Every challenge should tie to financial accuracy or career tracking, not arbitrary engagement.

📅

First Week Challenge: "Port Starter"

Log 5 shifts in your first 7 days.

Why it works: Data shows 6+ first-week shifts = 32% churn vs. 59% for 1 shift. Getting users to 5 shifts puts them past the activation threshold. The reward should be tangible: unlock pay comparison features or a "verified worker" badge on their profile.

Target All new users
Projected impact -15pp churn
Difficulty Easy to build
🔥

Shift Streak

Log a shift every work day you actually work. Track consecutive work-day logging streaks.

Why it works: Streaks create loss aversion. But unlike Duolingo, a port worker's streak should only count days they actually work, not calendar days. Use dispatch data or let them mark rest days. Show: "12-day logging streak — your longest is 34." Send a notification at 6 PM if they worked but haven't logged: "Don't break your streak."

Target Active users
Projected impact +15% engagement
Difficulty Medium
💰

Monthly Pay Audit Challenge

Verify every shift against your pay stub this month. Complete = "Pay Verified" month badge.

Why it works: This is the core value prop turned into a habit loop. When users verify their pay stubs and find (or confirm) accuracy, they're reinforcing why the app matters. Show a monthly audit score: "23/25 shifts verified. 2 outstanding." Those 2 remaining become pull-back triggers. Users who fully verify get to see: "You've confirmed $4,200 in accurate pay this month."

Target Regular + Power
Projected impact -10pp churn
Difficulty Medium
🎯

Seniority Tracker Challenge (Casuals)

Track your qualifying days toward the next seniority level. Visual progress bar.

Why it works: Casual workers need X qualifying days for advancement. This is life-changing career progression. Make it visible: "142 / 200 qualifying days — 71% to next level." Weekly update: "You logged 4 qualifying days this week. At this pace, you'll reach the next level by August." This gives casuals a reason to open the app even on days they don't work (to check progress).

Target Casual workers
Projected impact High retention
Difficulty Medium-High

Quarterly Earnings Milestone

Celebrate pay milestones: $10K, $25K, $50K, $100K tracked.

Why it works: Your average active user has $54K+ in tracked pay (based on ~$570/shift average). These are real, meaningful numbers. "You've tracked $50,000 in earnings with PORTPAL" is a flex-worthy moment that users will screenshot and share. This doubles as organic marketing. Make it shareable — a card they can post to social media or send to co-workers.

Target All users
Projected impact Viral loops

Referral Challenge: "Bring a Crew Member"

Invite a co-worker. Both get 1 month free Pro (when paywall launches).

Why it works: Longshoremen work in crews. They talk at dispatch. Word-of-mouth is your #1 channel in this community. Formalize it: when a user refers someone and that person logs 5 shifts, both get rewarded. Show a referral leaderboard: "Top referrers this month." Target power users (307 of them) — they're your most credible advocates. If each power user refers just 1 person, that's 307 new users.
