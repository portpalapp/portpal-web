## 1. Understanding Our Users

**Who are PORTPAL users?**

- BC port workers (longshoremen, foremen, checkers, equipment operators)
- Age range typically 25-60
- Working rotating shifts (day, night, graveyard)
- Smart, practical, no-nonsense people
- May not be "tech people" but can figure things out with clear instructions
- Value directness and honesty over corporate speak
- Time is money - literally. They want quick answers.

**Key insight:** These folks work hard, physical jobs. When they reach out for support, they want the problem solved, not a runaround. Respect their time and intelligence.

## 2. Support Channels

### Primary Channels

| Channel | Priority | Response Target | Best For |
|---------|----------|-----------------|----------|
| **In-App Chat** | HIGH | < 2 hours (business hours) | Quick questions, active issues |
| **Email** (support@portpal.app) | MEDIUM | < 24 hours | Complex issues, documentation needed |
| **Help Center/FAQ** | SELF-SERVICE | Immediate | Common questions, how-tos |

### Secondary Channels

| Channel | Priority | Response Target | Best For |
|---------|----------|-----------------|----------|
| **Community Forum** | LOW | < 48 hours | Feature discussions, tips sharing |
| **Social Media DMs** | MEDIUM | < 24 hours | General inquiries, redirect to proper channel |

### NOT Offered (and why)

- **Phone support:** Not scalable for a small team. Most issues can be resolved faster in writing where we can include screenshots and links.
- **WhatsApp/Text:** Security concerns, no audit trail.

### Business Hours

- **Core support:** Monday-Friday, 7 AM - 7 PM Pacific
- **Emergency only:** Weekends (billing issues, login problems)
- **Note:** Port workers work all shifts. Our 7 AM - 7 PM window catches people before day shift and after night shift.

## 3. FAQ Content

### Account & Login

**Q1: How do I create an account?**
> Download PORTPAL from the App Store or Google Play. Tap "Sign Up" and enter your email and create a password. You'll get a verification email - click the link to activate your account. That's it.

**Q2: I forgot my password. How do I reset it?**
> On the login screen, tap "Forgot Password?" Enter your email address and we'll send you a reset link. Check your spam folder if you don't see it within a few minutes. The link expires in 24 hours.

**Q3: Can I change my email address?**
> Yes. Go to Settings > Account > Email. Enter your new email and confirm with your password. You'll need to verify the new email before the change takes effect.

**Q4: How do I delete my account?**
> Go to Settings > Account > Delete Account. This will permanently remove all your data. If you have an active premium subscription, cancel it first to avoid being charged. Account deletion is final - we can't recover your data.

**Q5: Can I use PORTPAL on multiple devices?**
> Yes. Log in with the same email on any device. Your data syncs automatically. You can use it on your phone at work and tablet at home.

### Shift Entry

**Q6: How do I log a shift?**
> Tap the "+" button on the home screen. Select your job, location, and shift type (day/night/graveyard). The app will pre-fill standard hours - adjust if your shift was different. Tap Save.

**Q7: What if I worked overtime?**
> When entering your shift, tap the "Overtime" section and enter your OT hours. Choose whether it was time-and-a-half or double time. The app calculates your pay accordingly.

**Q8: I made a mistake on a shift. Can I edit it?**
> Yes. Find the shift in your history, tap on it, and hit "Edit." Make your changes and save. If the shift is more than 30 days old, you'll need to contact support.

**Q9: How do I add a subjob (like Rail or Ship)?**
> When entering a shift, after selecting your main job (like Tractor Trailer), tap "Subjob" and select from the list. This affects your hours and pay calculation.

**Q10: Why are my hours pre-filled? Can I change them?**
> We pre-fill based on standard hours for your job/shift combination. If your actual hours were different, just tap the hours field and change it. Always enter what you actually worked.

### Pay Calculations

**Q11: How does PORTPAL calculate my pay?**
> We use BCMEA rates (updated with each contract). Your pay = base rate + job differential, multiplied by your hours. Overtime uses time-and-a-half or double-time rates depending on when you worked.

**Q12: My calculated pay doesn't match my stub. What's wrong?**
> Common reasons: (1) Deductions aren't shown - we show gross pay, not net. (2) Different OT breakdown than expected. (3) Special situation not captured. Compare the hours and rate we show to your stub. If those match but total doesn't, the difference is likely deductions.

**Q13: When do pay rates update?**
> Rates update when BCMEA publishes new rates (typically April 1 each year). We update the app within 48 hours of official publication. You'll see a notification when rates change.

**Q14: What's a job differential?**
> Some jobs pay more per hour than base longshoreman rate. For example, Rubber Tire Gantry adds $1.00/hour, Tractor Trailer adds $0.65/hour. The app includes this automatically.

**Q15: Why is graveyard shift pay higher?**
> Graveyard shifts have a higher base rate to compensate for the tough hours. It's part of the BCMEA contract. The app uses the correct graveyard rate automatically.

### Premium & Billing

**Q16: What's the difference between free and premium?**
> **Free:** Track shifts, view basic pay calculations, 30-day history.
> **Premium:** Unlimited history, detailed pay analytics, export to spreadsheet, tax summaries, family sharing, priority support.

**Q17: How much does premium cost?**
> $4.99/month or $39.99/year (save 33% with annual). Prices in CAD.

**Q18: How do I cancel my subscription?**
> Go to Settings > Subscription > Cancel. Your premium access continues until the end of your current billing period. Cancellation is immediate - no waiting on hold, no talking to anyone.

**Q19: I was charged but didn't mean to subscribe. Can I get a refund?**
> If you subscribed in the last 48 hours and haven't used premium features, contact us for a refund. After 48 hours, refunds are handled by Apple/Google through their normal process. We can help you navigate that.

**Q20: Does premium auto-renew?**
> Yes, unless you cancel. You'll get a reminder email 7 days before renewal. Cancel anytime in Settings.

### Technical Issues

**Q21: The app is slow or freezing. What do I do?**
> Try these: (1) Close and reopen the app. (2) Check your internet connection. (3) Update to the latest version. (4) Restart your phone. If still slow, contact support with your phone model and app version.

**Q22: I'm not getting notifications. How do I fix this?**
> Check your phone settings - make sure notifications are enabled for PORTPAL. On iPhone: Settings > Notifications > PORTPAL. On Android: Settings > Apps > PORTPAL > Notifications.

**Q23: The app crashed. Did I lose my data?**
> No. Your data is saved to the cloud after every action. If the app crashes, just reopen it and your data should be there. If anything looks missing, contact support.

**Q24: Can I use PORTPAL offline?**
> You can view your existing shifts offline. To add or edit shifts, you need an internet connection. Changes sync automatically when you're back online.

**Q25: What phones does PORTPAL work on?**
> iPhone: iOS 14 or later. Android: Version 10 or later. Works on tablets too.

**Template Response:**
```

Let's figure this out. A few things to check:

1. Are you comparing our number to your gross pay (before deductions) or net pay (after deductions)? We show gross - your stub shows net.

   - Is the shift type (day/night/graveyard) correct?
```

## 5. Escalation Paths

### Tier 1: Self-Service / Bot
- FAQ lookups
- Password reset
- Basic "how do I" questions
- Status page for outages

**Handled by:** Help Center, in-app prompts, automated responses

**Escalate to Tier 2 if:** User says FAQ didn't help, or issue not in FAQ

---

### Tier 2: Human Support
- Account issues requiring verification
- Pay calculation questions
- Subscription problems
- Bug reports
- Feature requests
- Anything Tier 1 couldn't resolve

**Handled by:** Support team members

**Escalate to Tier 3 if:**
- Technical bug confirmed and reproducible
- Billing system error
- Data integrity issue
- User threatening legal action
- Security concern

---

### Tier 3: Technical / Billing / Escalation
- Server/infrastructure issues
- Database problems
- Payment processor issues
- Complex billing disputes
- Legal/compliance matters
- BCMEA rate discrepancies

**Handled by:** Engineering team, Founders, Legal (if applicable)

**Response expectation:** Within 4 hours for P1, 24 hours for P2

---

### Escalation Triggers - Immediate Tier 3

- "I'm a lawyer / this is a legal matter"
- Any mention of security breach or hacked account
- Data loss affecting multiple users
- App completely down for all users
- BCMEA rate is provably wrong
- User is a union representative with systemic concern

## 6. Tone & Voice Guidelines

### How to Talk to Longshoremen

**DO:**
- Be direct. Get to the point.
- Use plain language. No jargon.
- Acknowledge their expertise. They know their job better than you.
- Be honest about limitations. "I don't know, but I'll find out."
- Respect their time. Short sentences, clear steps.
- Match their energy. If they're casual, be casual. If they're frustrated, be understanding but solution-focused.

**DON'T:**
- Use corporate speak ("We value your feedback and are committed to...")
- Over-apologize ("I'm SO sorry for any inconvenience this may have caused...")
- Talk down or over-explain simple things
- Use emojis excessively (one or two is fine, a wall of emojis is not)
- Make promises you can't keep
- Blame the user

### Example: Good vs. Bad Responses

**User:** "This app charged me twice for premium."

**BAD Response:**
> Hi there! Thank you so much for reaching out to us today! We truly value you as a customer and are so sorry to hear you're experiencing this issue. Our team is committed to providing the best possible experience and we sincerely apologize for any inconvenience this may have caused. I will escalate this to our billing department and someone will be in touch within 3-5 business days. Thank you for your patience and understanding!

**GOOD Response:**
> That shouldn't happen - let me look into it right now. Can you send me the email on your account? I'll check the billing system and get one of those charges refunded today.

---

**User:** "The night shift rate is wrong."

**BAD Response:**
> According to our database, the rate displayed is the current BCMEA rate for night shifts. The rate is calculated using the official published rates and should be accurate. Please double-check your shift details.

**GOOD Response:**
> Let me check. What rate are you seeing, and what's on your pay stub? I'll compare against the BCMEA sheet. If we're wrong, we'll fix it.

---

**User:** "How the hell do I add overtime on this thing?"

**BAD Response:**
> I understand technology can be frustrating! Let me help you with that. To add overtime hours, please follow these simple steps: First, navigate to the shift entry screen by tapping the plus icon in the bottom navigation bar. Next, scroll down to locate the overtime section...

**GOOD Response:**
> When you're entering a shift, scroll down a bit - there's an "Overtime" section. Put your OT hours there and pick 1.5x or 2x. That's it.

---

### Handling Frustration

When a user is clearly frustrated:

1. **Acknowledge** - "I hear you. That's frustrating."
2. **Don't argue** - Even if they're wrong about something, start with their experience.
3. **Get to fixing** - "Let me see what's going on here."
4. **Follow up** - If it takes time to resolve, tell them when you'll update them and actually do it.

**Example:**

**User:** "I've been trying to fix this for an hour. Your app is broken and nobody's helping."

**Response:**
> That's too long to fight with an app. Tell me exactly what's happening - what are you trying to do and where does it go wrong? I'll get this sorted.

## 7. Pay Dispute Handling

Pay disputes are our most sensitive issues. Handle with care.

### Types of Pay Disputes

1. **App calculation doesn't match user's mental math**
   - Usually a misunderstanding about rates or OT rules
   - Walk through the calculation step by step

2. **App calculation doesn't match pay stub**
   - Could be app error, could be employer error, could be deductions
   - Compare line by line

3. **User believes they were underpaid by employer**
   - We do NOT resolve employer disputes
   - We CAN help them understand what they should have been paid

### Pay Dispute Process

**Step 1: Gather information**
```
- What job, shift, and location?
- What subjob (if applicable)?
- What hours (regular and OT)?
- What does the app show for pay?
- What does their stub show?
- What do they think it should be?
```

**Step 2: Verify our calculation**
- Check the rate table for that job/shift/day
- Verify any differential
- Confirm hours multiplied correctly
- Check for rounding issues

**Step 3: Compare to stub**
- Our number should match stub's gross pay (before deductions)
- If our number is higher: employer may have shorted them
- If our number is lower: we may have wrong rate/hours

**Step 4: Response**

**If app is correct and matches stub:**
> Your pay looks right based on the BCMEA rates. Here's the breakdown:
> - Base rate: $X
> - Differential: $X
> - Hours: X reg, X OT
> - Total: $X
>
> If you think it should be different, let me know what you're comparing to and I'll take another look.

**If app is wrong:**
> You're right - we had [the rate/the hours/the calculation] wrong. I'm fixing this now. [Explain what was wrong and what's being corrected.] Thanks for catching it.

**If employer may have shorted them:**
> Based on the BCMEA rates, your gross pay should be $X. Your stub shows $X. That's a $X difference.
>
> I'd recommend checking with your employer or union rep. We can't resolve pay disputes with employers, but you can screenshot the calculation from our app to show what you should have received.
>
> Want me to email you a breakdown you can bring to HR?

### Escalation for Pay Disputes

Escalate to Tier 3 if:
- Multiple users report same rate is wrong
- BCMEA rate appears incorrect in our system
- User is a union rep investigating systemic issue
- User mentions legal action

## 8. Feedback Collection

### Feature Requests

**How to collect:**
- Tag all feature requests in support system
- Include: user email, request summary, how they'd use it

**Response template:**
```
Good idea. I'm adding this to our feature request list. Can't promise when (or if) it'll happen, but the product team reviews these regularly. Thanks for the suggestion.
```

**Monthly process:**
- Support lead compiles top 10 requested features
- Shares with product team
- Product provides update on roadmap items

### Bug Reports

**Required information:**
- Steps to reproduce
- Expected behavior
- Actual behavior
- Device and OS version
- App version
- Screenshots/video if possible

**Response template:**
```
Got it. That's definitely a bug. I've logged it for the dev team. Can you tell me:
- What phone/tablet are you using?
- What version of the app? (Settings > About)
- Does it happen every time or sometimes?

That helps them track it down faster.
```

### Satisfaction Surveys

**When to send:**
- After ticket resolution (optional, don't spam)
- Quarterly to active users
- After subscription changes (cancel, upgrade, downgrade)

**Survey format:**
Keep it SHORT. One question is ideal.

> How would you rate your experience with PORTPAL support?
> [1-5 stars]
> [Optional comment box]

**Post-cancel survey:**
> Why did you cancel? (Optional, check all that apply)
> - Too expensive
> - Didn't use it enough
> - Found another app
> - Technical issues
> - Missing features I needed
> - Other: [text]

## 10. Support Metrics & Targets

### Response Time Targets

| Channel | First Response | Resolution |
|---------|---------------|------------|
| In-app chat | < 2 hours (business hours) | < 24 hours |
| Email | < 24 hours | < 48 hours |
| Social DM | < 24 hours | Redirect to proper channel |

### Quality Metrics

| Metric | Target | Measurement |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| First Contact Resolution | > 70% | Resolved without escalation |
| Customer Satisfaction | > 4.5/5 | Post-ticket survey |
| Escalation Rate | < 15% | Tickets escalated to Tier 3 |
| FAQ Deflection | > 40% | Users who read FAQ and don't contact |

### Volume Metrics (Track Weekly)

- Tickets opened
- Tickets closed
- Average handle time
- Tickets by category
- Tickets by tier

### Health Indicators

**Green (Healthy):**
- Response times meeting targets
- CSAT > 4.5
- No increase in complaint types

**Yellow (Watch):**
- Response times slipping
- CSAT 4.0-4.5
- Emerging pattern in complaints

**Red (Action Needed):**
- Response times missing targets
- CSAT < 4.0
- Same issue from multiple users
- Any security or data concern
