## Executive Summary

### Current Pricing
- **Pro Tier:** $99/year or $10/month
- **Free Tier:** Basic shift tracking features
- **Founding Member Offer:** $79/year (20% off)

### Key Findings

| Factor | Finding | Implication |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Target Market Income | $100K-$200K+ annually | Price sensitivity is LOW - value matters more than cost |
| Competitor Benchmark | Gridwise: $96/year ($9.99/mo) | PORTPAL pricing is market-aligned |
| Charm Pricing Effect | $99 vs $100 increases sales by 24-40% | Current $99 pricing is optimal |
| Annual Discount Sweet Spot | 15-20% off monthly | Current 17% discount ($99 vs $120) is ideal |
| Tiered Pricing Impact | 3-tier model increases revenue by 30% | Consider adding middle tier |

### Recommendation Summary

1. **Keep $99/year pricing** - perfectly positioned for market
2. **Add middle tier at $49/year** - capture price-sensitive segment
3. **Extend annual discount to 20%** - strengthen annual conversion
4. **Implement crew bulk pricing** - align with community structure
5. **Launch with 30% early-bird discount** - drive initial adoption

## Pricing Psychology Deep Dive

### 1. Charm Pricing ($99 vs $100)

**Research Findings:**
- Charm pricing increases sales by 24-40% (Thomas & Morwitz research)
- Left-digit effect: $99 "lives" in double digits, $100 is triple digits
- 60% of consumer products use charm pricing

**Application to PORTPAL:**
- **$99/year** is correct (not $100)
- **$9.99/month** would be slightly better than $10/month
- Consider: $79.99 founding member (vs $79)

*Source: [Capital One Shopping Research](https://capitaloneshopping.com/research/pricing-psychology-statistics/)*

**Recommendation:** Adjust monthly from $10 to $9.99 for consistency with charm pricing principle.

### 2. Annual vs Monthly Psychology

**Key Psychological Principles:**

| Principle | How It Works | PORTPAL Application |
|-----------|--------------|---------------------|
| Temporal Discounting | Humans prefer immediate rewards | Monthly feels "easier" short-term |
| Commitment Bias | Larger investment = higher engagement | Annual users churn 30-40% less |
| Mental Accounting | Spread costs feel smaller | Monthly = "coffee money" framing |
| Sunk Cost Effect | Annual users justify by using more | Increases feature adoption |

**Optimal Annual Discount:**
- Research shows 15-20% is the sweet spot
- Current: $99/year vs $120/year (monthly) = 17.5% discount
- This is optimal - within the ideal range

**Discount Framing Options:**

| Framing | Example | Psychological Effect |
|---------|---------|---------------------|
| Percentage Off | "Save 17%" | Standard, expected |
| Months Free | "Get 2 months FREE" | Feels like a gift |
| Daily Cost | "$0.27/day" | Trivializes expense |
| Comparison | "Less than one coffee/week" | Relatable anchor |

**Recommendation:** Use "Get 2 months FREE with annual" instead of "Save $21"

*Source: [RevenueCat Blog](https://www.revenuecat.com/blog/growth/subscription-pricing-psychology-how-to-influence-purchasing-decisions/)*

### 3. Price Anchoring

**Strategy:** Show higher price first to make target price feel like a deal.

**PORTPAL Implementation:**

```
Option A: Show monthly first (current)
Monthly: $10/month [$120/year]
Annual: $99/year [SAVE $21]

Option B: Value anchoring (recommended)
"Value delivered: $1,000+/year"
"Your price: Just $99/year"
"That's $0.27/day - less than a coffee"
```

**Recommendation:** Add value anchoring above pricing table. Quantify the value before showing price.

### 4. Decoy Pricing

**Theory:** Adding a less attractive middle option makes the target option look better.

**Classic Example:**
- Small: $3
- Medium: $6.50 (decoy - bad value)
- Large: $7 (target - great value vs medium)

**PORTPAL Application:**
If implementing 3 tiers, the middle tier should make Pro look like the obvious choice.

### 5. Trial Length Psychology

**Research Findings:**
- Shorter trials (7-day) often convert better than longer (30-day)
- Sweet spot for moderately complex apps: 14-21 days
- Current 14-day trial is well-positioned
- Extending to 30 days for referrals is smart (creates urgency differential)

*Source: [Ordway Labs](https://ordwaylabs.com/blog/saas-free-trial-length-conversion/)*

**Recommendation:** Keep 14-day standard trial. 30-day for referrals creates differentiation.

## Pricing Tier Options Analysis

### Option A: Current Model (Simple Two-Tier)

```
FREE                    PRO - $99/year
- Basic tracking        - Everything
- Limited features      - All features
```

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Simple to understand | No middle ground for hesitant users |
| Clear upgrade path | Leaves money on table from budget-conscious |
| Easy to maintain | All-or-nothing decision |
| No confusion | No upsell path within paid tiers |

**Revenue Model:**
- 600 users x 40% conversion = 240 Pro @ $99 = **$23,760/year**

---

### Option B: Good-Better-Best (Three-Tier)

```
FREE                    PLUS - $49/year           PRO - $99/year
- Basic tracking        - Everything in Free      - Everything in Plus
- Weekly AI (1x)        - Unlimited AI chat       - Pay stub reconciliation
- Basic dashboard       - Templates               - Daily job predictions
                        - Advanced analytics      - Priority support
                        - Data export             - Early feature access
```

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Captures mid-market segment | More complex to communicate |
| 60-70% typically choose middle tier | Feature allocation challenges |
| 30% revenue lift (research) | Support for multiple tiers |
| Natural upsell path | Could cannibalize Pro tier |

**Revenue Model:**
- 600 users
- Free: 30% (180 users) = $0
- Plus: 40% (240 users) @ $49 = $11,760
- Pro: 30% (180 users) @ $99 = $17,820
- **Total: $29,580/year** (+24% vs current model)

*Source: [Maxio Blog](https://www.maxio.com/blog/tiered-pricing-examples-for-saas-businesses)*

**Feature Allocation Strategy:**

The key differentiator for Pro should be **pay stub reconciliation** - this is the highest-value feature and justifies the premium.

| Feature | Free | Plus | Pro |
|---------|------|------|-----|
| Shift logging | Unlimited | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Auto rate calc | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Pension tracking | Basic | Advanced | Advanced |
| AI questions | 1/week | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Callback feature | No | Yes | Yes |
| Templates | No | Yes | Yes |
| Analytics | Basic | Full | Full |
| Data export | No | Yes | Yes |
| Pay stub upload | No | No | Yes |
| AI reconciliation | No | No | Yes |
| Discrepancy alerts | No | No | Yes |
| Job predictions | 1/week | 3/week | Daily |
| Priority support | No | No | Yes |

---

### Option C: Usage-Based Pricing

```
FREE (up to 10 shifts/month)    PRO (unlimited) - $99/year
- All features                   - Unlimited shifts
- Limited to 10 shifts           - Priority support
```

| Pros | Cons |
|------|------|
| Natural for casual users | Confusing for steady workers |
| Clear value = usage | Most longshoremen work 15-20+ shifts/month |
| Easy to understand limit | Would force most users to pay |
| No feature gating | Doesn't reward feature adoption |

**Analysis:** Usage-based makes sense for variable-use products. Longshoremen work consistently (15-25 shifts/month), so almost everyone would exceed free limits immediately. This feels punitive rather than fair.

**Recommendation:** NOT recommended for PORTPAL. The user base has consistent, predictable usage.

---

### Option D: Hybrid (Recommended)

```
FREE                    PLUS - $49/year           PRO - $99/year
Basic features          Core features             Everything + AI
```

**The Twist:** Position PLUS as the "good value" tier and PRO as the "AI-powered" tier.

| Tier | Positioning | Target User |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Free | Try it out | Curious/skeptical |
| Plus | Everything you need | Value-conscious worker |
| Pro | AI superpowers | Tech-forward, optimization-focused |

**Why This Works:**
1. Free users can upgrade to Plus without big commitment
2. Plus users get real value, become loyal
3. Pro is clearly differentiated by AI features
4. Avoids "feature withholding" perception

---

### Tier Recommendation

**Primary Recommendation: Option B (Three-Tier) with modifications**

**Reasoning:**
1. Research shows 30% revenue lift with 3 tiers
2. Middle tier captures hesitant buyers
3. Creates natural upsell path
4. Aligns with competitive landscape (Gridwise has 2, opportunity to differentiate)

**Suggested Structure:**

| Tier | Price | Annual Savings | Positioning |
|------|-------|----------------|-------------|
| Free | $0 | - | "Try the basics" |
| Plus | $49/year ($4.99/mo) | 17% | "Best value for most workers" |
| Pro | $99/year ($9.99/mo) | 17% | "AI-powered pay protection" |

**Naming Alternatives:**
- Free / Standard / Pro
- Basic / Plus / Premium
- Starter / Worker / Pro
- Free / Essential / Complete

**Recommended Names:** Free / Plus / Pro (simple, clear hierarchy)

## A/B Testing Roadmap

### Test Priority Matrix

| Test | Priority | Expected Impact | Sample Size Needed | Duration |
|------|----------|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| Charm pricing ($9.99 vs $10) | Medium | 5-10% lift | 500+ per variant | 30 days |
| 2-tier vs 3-tier | High | 20-30% revenue lift | 1000+ per variant | 60 days |
| Annual framing ($ vs months free) | High | 10-15% annual conversion | 500+ per variant | 30 days |
| Trial length (14 vs 7 day) | Medium | 5-15% conversion | 500+ per variant | 45 days |
| Founding member deadline | Low | 5% urgency lift | 300+ per variant | 14 days |

*Source: [Monetizely](https://www.getmonetizely.com/articles/how-to-design-your-first-saas-pricing-ab-test-a-data-driven-approach)*

### Test 1: Monthly Charm Pricing

**Hypothesis:** $9.99/month will outperform $10/month due to left-digit effect

**Variants:**
- A: $10/month (control)
- B: $9.99/month (test)

**Primary Metric:** Monthly subscription selection rate
**Secondary Metrics:** Overall conversion, revenue per user

**Sample Size:** 500 visitors per variant (1,000 total)
**Duration:** 30 days minimum
**Expected Lift:** 5-10%

### Test 2: Two-Tier vs Three-Tier

**Hypothesis:** Adding a $49 Plus tier will increase overall revenue by 20-30%

**Variants:**
- A: Free + Pro only (control)
- B: Free + Plus ($49) + Pro (test)

**Primary Metric:** Revenue per visitor
**Secondary Metrics:** Conversion rate by tier, tier distribution

**Sample Size:** 1,000 visitors per variant (2,000 total)
**Duration:** 60 days (to capture full decision cycles)
**Expected Lift:** 20-30% revenue increase

**Risk Mitigation:** If Plus cannibalizes Pro significantly, adjust feature allocation

### Test 3: Annual Discount Framing

**Hypothesis:** "Get 2 months FREE" will outperform "Save $21" for annual conversions

**Variants:**
- A: "Save $21" (control)
- B: "Get 2 months FREE" (test)
- C: "$0.27/day" (test)

**Primary Metric:** Annual vs monthly selection rate
**Secondary Metrics:** Overall conversion, churn at renewal

**Sample Size:** 500 per variant (1,500 total)
**Duration:** 30 days
**Expected Lift:** 10-15% increase in annual selection

### Test 4: Trial Length

**Hypothesis:** 7-day trial will have equal or higher conversion than 14-day due to urgency

**Variants:**
- A: 14-day trial (control)
- B: 7-day trial (test)

**Primary Metric:** Trial-to-paid conversion rate
**Secondary Metrics:** Feature adoption during trial, time to conversion

**Sample Size:** 500 trial starts per variant (1,000 total)
**Duration:** 45 days (to capture full conversion window)
**Expected Lift:** Potentially 10-20% conversion improvement

**Risk:** May reduce conversion if product requires learning time

### Test 5: Value Anchor Messaging

**Hypothesis:** Showing "$X in errors caught" will increase conversion vs generic messaging

**Variants:**
- A: "Track your shifts" (control)
- B: "Catch $127 in average errors/year" (test)
- C: "One caught error pays for a year" (test)

**Primary Metric:** Pricing page conversion rate
**Secondary Metrics:** Click-through rate, time on page

**Sample Size:** 500 per variant (1,500 total)
**Duration:** 30 days
**Expected Lift:** 15-25% conversion improvement

### Testing Tools

**Recommended:**
- Optimizely (enterprise, robust)
- VWO (mid-market, easy)
- Google Optimize (free, limited)
- PostHog (open source, growing)

**For PORTPAL (early stage):** Start with Google Optimize or PostHog, migrate to VWO as scale increases.

### Sample Size Calculator Reference

**Rule of Thumb:**
- 5% minimum detectable effect (MDE) = ~1,000 per variant
- 10% MDE = ~400 per variant
- 20% MDE = ~100 per variant

**For pricing tests:** Aim for 5% MDE (high stakes), so plan for 1,000+ per variant.

**Duration:** Always run for at least one full business cycle (2-4 weeks minimum), never more than 60 days.

*Source: [Speero A/B Calculator](https://speero.com/ab-test-calculator)*
