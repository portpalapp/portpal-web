## Strategic Expansion Paths

### Option A: "ILWU First" (Recommended)
**Logic:** Same union = similar contracts, pay structures, terminology

```
Phase 1: BC (Year 1)
├── Local 500 Vancouver ✓ (current)
├── Local 502 New Westminster
├── Local 505 Prince Rupert
└── Local 508 Chemainus

Phase 2: Pacific Northwest (Year 2)
├── Local 23 Tacoma (2,000)
├── Local 19 Seattle (1,500)
└── Local 8 Portland (700)

Phase 3: California (Year 3)
├── Local 13 LA/Long Beach (7,500) ← BIGGEST PRIZE
├── Local 10 San Francisco
└── Local 63 Marine Clerks LA

Phase 4: Other ILWU (Year 4)
├── Hawaii Local 142
├── Alaska Local 200
└── Smaller CA locals
```

**Pros:**
- ILWU contracts are similar across regions
- Pay calculation engine transfers with minimal changes
- Union relationships can cascade (ILWU International endorsement)
- ~24,000 workers with one union relationship

**Cons:**
- Skips Canadian expansion (Montreal)
- ILA is larger but requires separate contract knowledge

---

### Option B: "Canada First"
**Logic:** Same country = same currency, regulations, tax implications

```
Phase 1: BC (Year 1)
├── All ILWU Canada locals (7,200)

Phase 2: Eastern Canada (Year 2)
├── Montreal (ILA) (~1,500)
├── Halifax (~500)
└── Other Atlantic ports

Phase 3: US West Coast (Year 3)
├── Seattle/Tacoma
├── Portland
└── California

Phase 4: US East Coast (Year 4)
├── NY/NJ
├── Southeast ports
```

**Pros:**
- Single currency (CAD) for longer
- Canadian privacy law (PIPEDA) consistency
- Potential for Canadian government/union partnerships
- Smaller ILA entry point (Montreal) before big US markets

**Cons:**
- Montreal ILA is different union, different contracts
- Smaller total market in Canada (~9,000 vs ~52,000 US)
- May miss ILWU momentum by switching unions

---

### Option C: "Maximum Impact"
**Logic:** Go straight to biggest markets after proving BC

```
Phase 1: BC (Year 1)
├── Local 500 + 502 (5,740)

Phase 2: LA/Long Beach (Year 2)
├── Local 13 (7,500) ← Largest single local in North America
├── Local 63 Marine Clerks

Phase 3: NY/NJ (Year 3)
├── All ILA locals (~3,700)

Phase 4: Fill in gaps (Year 4)
├── Seattle, Portland, other ILWU
├── Charleston, Jacksonville, Gulf
```

**Pros:**
- Faster path to scale
- Investor-friendly growth story
- LA/Long Beach is strategic (busiest port in North America)

**Cons:**
- Skips natural geographic expansion
- LA/Long Beach is competitive (Gridwise, others may target)
- Higher marketing cost in large markets

---

### Option D: "West Coast Corridor"
**Logic:** Geographic proximity, all ILWU, natural travel/word-of-mouth

```
Year 1: BC
├── Vancouver (500, 502)
├── Prince Rupert (505)

Year 2: Pacific Northwest
├── Seattle (19)
├── Tacoma (23)
├── Portland (8)

Year 3: Northern California
├── San Francisco (10)
├── Oakland (10)
├── Stockton (54)

Year 4: Southern California
├── LA/Long Beach (13)
├── San Diego (29)
├── Port Hueneme (46)
```

**Pros:**
- Natural geographic flow (workers may move between ports)
- All ILWU = consistent pay structures
- Build density before spreading thin

**Cons:**
- Slower to reach biggest market (LA)
- Ignores Canadian East Coast opportunity

## Market Entry Requirements by Region

### ILWU Markets (BC, WA, OR, CA, HI, AK)
- Pay calculation engine: 80% transferable
- Job classifications: Similar across all ILWU
- Changes needed: Local-specific locations, minor rate differences
- Marketing: "Same union, same app"

### ILA Markets (East Coast, Gulf, Montreal)
- Pay calculation engine: 40% transferable
- Job classifications: Different terminology
- Changes needed: New pay rule configurations, different OT rules
- Marketing: Needs separate approach

### Configuration Effort by Market

| Market | Config Effort | Dev Time | Notes |
|--------|---------------|----------|-------|
| ILWU Canada (any) | Low | 1-2 days | Same as current |
| ILWU US (any) | Medium | 1 week | Similar but USD, different rates |
| ILA (any) | High | 2-3 weeks | Different contract structure |

## Union Relationship Strategy

### ILWU Approach
1. **Local 500/502 success stories** → share with ILWU Canada leadership
2. **Request ILWU Canada endorsement** → letter of support
3. **Present to ILWU International** → potential partnership
4. **Cascade to US ILWU locals** → "Canadian brothers use it"

### ILA Approach (Later)
1. **Start with Montreal** → smaller, Canadian, test ILA waters
2. **Learn ILA contract structure** → adapt pay engine
3. **Target progressive locals first** → Charleston, Jacksonville
4. **Avoid NY/NJ initially** → politically complex

## Summary: Why ILWU-First Wins

1. **Contract Similarity** - ILWU contracts are negotiated together, pay structures similar
2. **Union Network** - Get ILWU Canada to vouch for you to ILWU US
3. **Technical Efficiency** - Pay engine transfers with minimal changes
4. **Geographic Flow** - BC → Seattle → LA is natural corridor
5. **Competitive Timing** - ILA markets less urgent, can learn from ILWU

**Recommended Path:**
```
BC (Y1) → Seattle/Tacoma (Y2) → LA/Long Beach (Y3) → Montreal (Y4) → ILA East (Y5)
```
