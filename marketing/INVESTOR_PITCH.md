## 2. THE PROBLEM

### Complexity That Costs Workers Money

Port workers face a pay system so complex that even payroll departments get it wrong:

| Complexity Factor | Scale |
|-------------------|-------|
| Job classifications | 42 different jobs |
| Pay differentials | 5 classification tiers (+$0.00 to +$2.50/hr) |
| Shift variations | Day, Night, Graveyard with different multipliers |
| Location modifiers | Different hours by terminal |
| Weekend/Holiday rates | 3-4 different rate tiers |
| Total rate combinations | **990+ unique pay scenarios** |

### The Pay Error Problem

Longshoremen report that payroll errors are common:

- **Day shift worker** expects $53.17/hr but should get $55.67/hr for HD Mechanic differential
- **Night shift TT driver at Centennial** works 9 hours but gets paid for 8
- **Saturday rates** at 1.28x are often miscalculated

**The math:** A single missed hour at night shift rates costs a worker $67-87. One error per month = $800-1,000/year in lost wages.

### The Time Waste Problem

Without a tracking app, workers:
- Manually calculate expected pay using paper rate sheets
- Wait for pay stubs then reconcile by hand
- File grievances through union reps (weeks of delay)
- Often give up on small discrepancies

**Average time spent:** 15-30 minutes per shift on manual tracking. That's **100+ hours/year** for active workers.

### Market Pain Quantified

| Pain Point | Impact |
|------------|--------|
| Payroll errors (estimated) | 5-10% of shifts have some discrepancy |
| Average error value | $50-200 per occurrence |
| Worker time lost annually | 100+ hours in tracking/reconciliation |
| Grievance resolution time | 2-8 weeks average |

## 3. THE SOLUTION

### What PORTPAL Does

**Core Value Proposition:** Log your shift in 30 seconds, know exactly what you should be paid, catch errors before they become grievances.

**How It Works:**

1. **Select Job + Location + Shift** - Pre-populated dropdowns, no typing required
2. **Auto-Calculate Pay** - App knows all 990 rate combinations, calculates instantly
3. **Track Over Time** - Historical records, tax-ready reports, pay stub matching
4. **Flag Discrepancies** - When pay stub arrives, compare actual vs. expected

### Key Differentiators

| Feature | PORTPAL | Generic Time Tracker | Spreadsheet |
|---------|---------|---------------------|-------------|
| Port-specific job classifications | Yes | No | Manual |
| BCMEA rate tables built-in | Yes | No | Manual lookup |
| Location-based hour rules | Yes | No | Manual |
| Weekend/holiday auto-detection | Yes | No | Manual |
| Mobile-first design | Yes | Varies | No |
| Offline capable | Yes | Varies | Sort of |

### Product Screenshots (Described)

**Home Screen:** Clean list of recent shifts with job, location, and calculated pay. One-tap to add new shift.

**Add Shift Screen:** Three dropdowns (Job, Location, Shift), auto-populated hours based on known terminal rules. Optional notes field. "Calculate Pay" button shows breakdown.

**Pay Breakdown Screen:** Shows base rate, differential, regular hours, OT hours, gross pay. Visual confirmation before saving.

**History Screen:** Monthly/yearly totals, exportable for taxes. Pay stub matching checklist.

### Local-by-Local Approach

Each union local has:
- Slightly different contract terms
- Different cultural dynamics
- Different rate structures

**Our approach:**
1. Partner with 2-3 early adopters in new local
2. Build/customize rate engine for their contract
3. Seed with beta users for validation
4. Launch with local-specific marketing
5. Let word-of-mouth take over

**Why this works:** Longshoremen are skeptical of outsiders. Local champions are essential.

### Viral Mechanics

| Mechanic | How It Works |
|----------|--------------|
| Pay error stories | "This app caught a $400 error" spreads fast |
| Dispatch hall chatter | Workers see each other daily, share tips |
| Union meetings | Product demos at local meetings |
| Referral incentives | Free premium months for referrals |
| Social proof | "5,000 shifts tracked by Local 500 members" |

**Estimated viral coefficient:** 0.4-0.6 (each user brings 0.5 new users on average through word-of-mouth).
