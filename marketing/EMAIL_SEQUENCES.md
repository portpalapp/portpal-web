## Email Design Guidelines

### Tone and Voice
- Direct and practical - longshoremen don't have time for marketing fluff
- Use "you" not "we" as much as possible
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- No corporate speak - write like you're talking to a coworker
- Acknowledge their reality (long shifts, physical work, complicated pay structures)

### Formatting
- Plain text style preferred (minimal HTML design)
- Mobile-first (most will read on phones during breaks)
- Single clear CTA per email
- Keep total length under 200 words when possible

### Subject Lines
- Under 50 characters
- No clickbait - say what the email is about
- Use action words: "Log," "Check," "Your," "Quick"
- Avoid spam triggers: "FREE," "ACT NOW," "LIMITED TIME"

### Testing Framework
- Always run A/B tests on subject lines
- Test send times: Early morning (before shift) vs. Evening (after shift)
- Track: Open rate, click rate, conversion rate
- Goal open rates: 30%+ for transactional, 20%+ for marketing

## Technical Implementation Notes

### Email Service Requirements
- Transactional email capability (receipts, notifications)
- Marketing automation (sequences, triggers)
- Segmentation by: subscription status, activity level, referral count
- A/B testing built-in
- Unsubscribe management

### Recommended Triggers
| Trigger | Sequence |
|---------|----------|
| New signup | Welcome sequence |
| Trial started | Trial-to-paid sequence |
| No app open 7+ days | Win-back sequence |
| 30 days before renewal | Renewal sequence |
| 10th shift logged | Referral ask |
| Referral converts | Referral reward |

### Suppression Rules
- Don't send marketing emails within 24 hours of transactional emails
- Pause win-back sequence if user opens app
- Never send more than 1 email per day
- Unsubscribe from marketing doesn't affect transactional
