## 6. TESTIMONIAL FORMAT

### Pre-Interview Prep Questions

**Warm-Up (off camera):**
1. How long have you been a longshoreman?
2. What got you into this work?
3. What terminal do you work at most often?

**Core Questions (on camera):**

**About the Problem:**
1. "Before PORTPAL, how did you track your shifts?"
2. "Walk me through what that actually looked like."
3. "Did you ever find errors in your pay?"
4. "How did that make you feel?"
5. "What was the hardest part about tracking everything?"

**About the Solution:**
6. "How did you first hear about PORTPAL?"
7. "What made you decide to try it?"
8. "Walk me through logging a shift. What does that look like?"
9. "What feature surprised you the most?"
10. "Have you caught any discrepancies since using it?"

**About the Transformation:**
11. "What's different now about how you track your earnings?"
12. "How has it changed your stress level around pay?"
13. "Would you recommend it to other workers? Why?"

**The Zinger (for soundbites):**
14. "If you had to describe PORTPAL in one sentence, what would you say?"
15. "What would you tell yourself a year ago about this app?"
16. "Fill in the blank: Before PORTPAL, I was ___. Now I'm ___."

### B-Roll Shot List

**Personal Setup (30 sec each):**
- Worker arriving at terminal
- Clocking in / checking dispatch
- Walking through terminal
- Operating equipment (appropriate distance)
- Checking phone during break
- Driving home

**Home/Personal Life (30 sec each):**
- At kitchen table with papers (recreating old method)
- Using app on phone (close-up of hands)
- Showing spouse/family the app
- Relaxed expression while checking progress

**Detail Shots (15 sec each):**
- Close-up of old notebook (if they have one)
- Close-up of pay stub
- Fingers tapping on phone screen
- App interface on screen
- Progress bar animation
- Checkmarks appearing

**Atmospheric (15 sec each):**
- Container cranes at golden hour
- Trucks moving through terminal
- Ships in port
- Workers walking together
- Safety equipment details

### Interview Setup Notes

**Location Options:**
1. At home (kitchen table - authentic)
2. At terminal (during break - work context)
3. Neutral indoor (union hall, quiet space)
4. In vehicle (casual, authentic energy)

**Technical:**
- Natural light preferred, supplement if needed
- Lapel mic for clear audio
- Subject looks slightly off-camera (interviewer eye line)
- Clean background, slight terminal/work hints okay
- Shoot in 4K for flexibility

**Tone Direction:**
- Conversational, not scripted
- Let them talk in their own words
- Encourage stories, not just statements
- Allow pauses - don't fill silences too fast
- Get genuine reactions, not performed ones

**PRODUCTION NOTES:**

**Screen Recording Tips:**
- Use actual app (or high-fidelity prototype)
- Record at 60fps for smooth scrolling
- Tap animations should be visible but not exaggerated
- Use finger/hand in frame for taps (feels more human)
- Blur or use placeholder numbers for privacy demonstration

**Voiceover Style:**
- Calm, confident, helpful
- Not salesy or urgent
- Slightly faster pace than corporate (real person energy)
- Male or female - match your target demographic testing

**Music:**
- Low, clean, modern electronic
- Not intrusive - should feel like background
- Slight build during calculation reveal
- Resolves cleanly at end

**Text Animations:**
- Clean, minimal, sans-serif font
- Appear on beat with VO
- White or brand blue
- Subtle fade in/out

**Thumbnail Concept:** Phone screen showing app interface with "30 SECONDS" text overlay

## Production Checklist

### Pre-Production
- [ ] Secure filming locations (terminal permission if needed)
- [ ] Book talent (real workers preferred)
- [ ] Prepare wardrobe (safety vests, work boots - authentic)
- [ ] Create shot list and schedule
- [ ] Brief all talent on messaging guidelines
- [ ] Prepare app for screen recording (clean data)

### Equipment
- [ ] Camera (4K capable)
- [ ] Lapel mics for interviews
- [ ] Boom for ambient
- [ ] Lighting kit (natural light supplement)
- [ ] Screen recording setup (phone mount)
- [ ] B-roll stabilizer/gimbal

### Post-Production
- [ ] Color grade consistently across all spots
- [ ] Sound mix (VO clear, music under)
- [ ] Add all text overlays per script
- [ ] Create multiple aspect ratios (16:9, 9:16, 1:1)
- [ ] Export multiple lengths where applicable
- [ ] Thumbnail design for each

### Compliance
- [ ] No visible terminal names/logos without permission
- [ ] Blur any unintentional faces
- [ ] Talent releases signed
- [ ] Music licensing confirmed
- [ ] App store approval (if showing app)

## Appendix: Quick Reference

### Key Messaging (Never Deviate)
- Focus on ACCURACY, not amounts
- Focus on COMPLEXITY, not salary
- Focus on TIME SAVINGS
- Focus on PEACE OF MIND
- Focus on FAIRNESS (getting what you earned)

### What NOT to Show/Say
- Large earnings numbers prominently displayed
- "This is what longshoremen make"
- Lifestyle content implying wealth
- Salary comparisons to other industries
- "Flex" energy of any kind

### Brand Voice
- Direct, not corporate
- Blue-collar authentic
- Confident but not arrogant
- Helpful, not preachy
- Union proud, worker solidarity

### Visual Style
- Industrial aesthetic with clean tech contrast
- Golden hour port footage
- Real workers, not models
- Authentic settings (kitchen tables, break rooms, trucks)
- App interface: clean, modern, minimal

### Music Guidelines
- Industrial ambient for problem segments
- Clean, confident for solution segments
- Avoid: corporate uplift, overly dramatic
- Match energy of working class, not Silicon Valley
