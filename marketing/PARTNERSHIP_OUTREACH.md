## 1. UNION OFFICIALS

### Initial Cold Outreach Email

**Subject Line Options:**
- "Helping [LOCAL NUMBER] members protect their paychecks"
- "A tool built BY longshoremen, FOR longshoremen"
- "Free resource for [LOCAL NUMBER] members - pay tracking app"

---

**Email Body:**

```
Subject: Helping [LOCAL NUMBER] members protect their paychecks

Dear [OFFICIAL NAME],

I'm reaching out because I believe [LOCAL NUMBER] members are leaving money on the table - and I'd like to help fix that.

I'm [YOUR NAME], a fellow longshoreman and the creator of PORTPAL, a free shift-tracking app designed specifically for our industry.

THE PROBLEM WE'RE SOLVING:

Every week, I hear the same story from workers at [TERMINAL NAMES]:
- "I think my check was short but I can't prove it"
- "I lost track of my hours and got surprised at tax time"
- "I don't know if I got the right differential for that shift"

Our complex pay structure - with differentials, shift premiums, overtime tiers, and varying rates by day of week - makes it nearly impossible to verify pay manually.

WHAT PORTPAL DOES:

- Tracks shifts with correct pay rates (Class 1-4 differentials built in)
- Calculates expected earnings based on BCMEA rates
- Alerts workers when their stub doesn't match their records
- Provides tax-ready earnings reports

WHY I'M CONTACTING THE UNION:

I'm not asking for money or formal endorsement. I simply want your feedback:

1. Would a tool like this be valuable to members?
2. Are there features that would make it more useful?
3. Would you be open to a brief call to discuss?

The app is free and will remain free for individual workers. I'm building this because I've seen too many of my brothers and sisters get shorted.

Happy to provide a demo at your convenience.

In solidarity,
[YOUR NAME]
[YOUR LOCAL]
[PHONE NUMBER]
[EMAIL]

P.S. - You can try the app yourself at [APP LINK]. Takes 30 seconds to log a shift.
```

### Objection Handling - Union Officials

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We don't endorse products" | "Totally understand. We're not asking for endorsement - just awareness. Would you be open to mentioning it exists so members can decide for themselves?" |
| "What's in it for you?" | "Honestly, I'm a longshoreman who got tired of second-guessing my paychecks. The app is free. If we ever offer premium features, they'll be optional. But core pay tracking will always be free." |
| "Members won't use it" | "You might be right! But the ones who do use it are catching pay errors. Even if only 10% of members use it, that's [X] workers protecting their earnings." |
| "We already have a process for pay disputes" | "Absolutely - and PORTPAL makes that process stronger. When a member files a grievance with detailed shift records and pay calculations, they have documentation to back up their claim." |
| "How do we know you won't sell member data?" | "Great question. We don't collect personal data beyond what users voluntarily log. No names, no PII. And we'll never share or sell any data. Happy to show you our privacy policy." |
| "The employer might not like this" | "The app doesn't accuse anyone of anything. It just helps workers keep accurate records. That's something everyone should support." |

## Objection Handling Guide

### Universal Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "What's the catch?" | "No catch. The app is free for workers and will stay free. We may add optional premium features later, but core shift tracking will always be free. I built this because I'm a longshoreman who got frustrated with the same problem." |
| "How do you make money?" | "Right now, we don't. We're focused on building something useful. Eventually, we may offer premium features or enterprise tools, but worker-facing features will stay free." |
| "I've never heard of you" | "That's fair - we're new! We have [X] users so far, mostly through word of mouth. I'm reaching out to grow awareness and get feedback. Would you like to try it yourself?" |

## Follow-Up Sequences

### Standard Follow-Up Timeline

| Day | Action | Template |
|-----|--------|----------|
| 0 | Initial outreach | Use category-specific template above |
| 5 | First follow-up | Short, friendly reminder |
| 10 | Second follow-up | Add social proof or new info |
| 30 | Third follow-up | Final check-in, leave door open |
| 60 | Re-engagement | New angle or update |

### Follow-Up Best Practices

1. **Keep follow-ups short** - 3-4 sentences max
2. **Add value each time** - new info, social proof, or different angle
3. **Don't be pushy** - "no response is a response"
4. **Track responses** - CRM or spreadsheet with dates and notes
5. **Personalize** - reference previous contact or their work
6. **Know when to stop** - 3 follow-ups with no response = pause for 60+ days
