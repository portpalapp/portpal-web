# PORTPAL Marketing Strategy - Revised
## Focus: The Problem, Not The Pay

---

## The Shift

**OLD APPROACH (Wrong):**
"Look what longshoremen make! $648/shift!"
→ Invites hate, resentment, "overpaid" comments

**NEW APPROACH (Right):**
"Is your pay correct? Most don't check. Here's why that's a problem."
→ Focuses on accuracy, fairness, the complexity of tracking

---

## Core Messaging Pillars

### 1. ACCURACY, NOT AMOUNT
- "Are you being paid correctly?"
- "The math is complicated. Mistakes happen."
- "Your employer has software. What do you have?"

### 2. COMPLEXITY
- 42 job types, each with different rules
- 24 terminals, some with different hours
- 3 shift types, weekend premiums, differentials
- "No one can track this in their head"

### 3. TIME & PEACE OF MIND
- "30 seconds to log vs 30 minutes of spreadsheets"
- "Know where you stand without the stress"
- "Stop wondering, start knowing"

### 4. FAIRNESS (Not Wealth)
- "Getting what you earned isn't greedy. It's fair."
- "Small errors add up. Over years."
- "The system is complex. That benefits who?"

---

## Visual Treatment of Numbers

### DO:
- Show **percentages** ("87% to goal")
- Show **progress bars** (visual, not numeric)
- Show **relative comparisons** ("+12% vs last week")
- Show **discrepancy amounts** ("Found: $34 short" - small, specific errors)
- **Blur/fade** large earnings numbers when shown
- Use **ranges** instead of specifics ("between X and Y")

### DON'T:
- Lead with big earnings numbers
- Show "This is what longshoremen make"
- Display weekly/annual totals prominently
- Create content that invites "you're overpaid" comments

## Revised Content Angles

### Angle 1: "Is Your Pay Right?"
**Hook:** "When's the last time you actually checked the math on your pay stub?"

**Story:** The system is complex. Different rates for different jobs, shifts, days, terminals. Your employer uses sophisticated software. You're using... what? A notebook? Your memory? Mistakes happen. Usually not in your favor.

**CTA:** "PORTPAL checks the math. So you don't have to."

---

### Angle 2: "The Complexity Problem"
**Hook:** "42 job types. 24 terminals. 3 shift types. Weekend premiums. Differentials. Who can track all that?"

**Story:** Longshore pay isn't simple. There are rules on top of rules. Even payroll makes mistakes - not because they're malicious, but because it's genuinely complicated. The question isn't whether errors happen. It's whether you catch them.

**CTA:** "One app that knows the rules."

---

### Angle 3: "The 30-Second Log"
**Hook:** "I used to spend 20 minutes a week on spreadsheets. Now it's 30 seconds."

**Story:** You pick the job, location, shift. The app knows the rates, the hours, the differentials. It calculates everything. You confirm and save. Done. That's it. No mental math. No hoping you remembered right.

**CTA:** "Your time is worth more than data entry."

---

### Angle 4: "Peace of Mind" (Pension Focus)
**Hook:** "Will I hit my goal this year? I used to stress about it every December."

**Story:** Now I open the app. See where I stand. See if I'm on pace. If I want to take time off, it tells me what that means for my timeline. No more guessing. No more end-of-year panic.

**Visual:** Progress bar at 73%, projected completion date. NO dollar amounts visible.

**CTA:** "Track your progress. Not your anxiety."

---

### Angle 5: "The Paper Problem"
**Hook:** "My dad's been doing this 30 years. Still uses a spiral notebook."

**Story:** Coffee stains. Crossed-out numbers. Pages falling out. And somehow he's supposed to audit his own pay from that? There's a better way. And it's not about being fancy - it's about being accurate.

**CTA:** "Upgrade from paper. Not because it's new - because it works."

---

### Angle 6: "Found Money" (Discrepancy Focus)
**Hook:** "Logged my shift. App flagged something. Checked my stub. They were wrong."

**Story:** Small errors. A few bucks here, a few there. Easy to miss. Easy to let slide. But over a year? Over a career? It adds up. The app caught it. I wouldn't have.

**Important:** Show SMALL discrepancy amounts ($12, $34). Not big earnings.

**CTA:** "Every dollar you earned. Accounted for."

## What We DON'T Do

1. ❌ "What longshoremen make" content
2. ❌ Salary transparency / "how much I earn"
3. ❌ Showing big weekly/annual numbers
4. ❌ Anything that invites "you're overpaid" discourse
5. ❌ Comparison to other jobs/industries
6. ❌ Lifestyle content implying wealth

## What We DO

1. ✅ Focus on ACCURACY and FAIRNESS
2. ✅ Highlight COMPLEXITY of the pay system
3. ✅ Show TIME SAVINGS
4. ✅ Emphasize PEACE OF MIND
5. ✅ Use percentages and progress bars
6. ✅ Show small discrepancies caught
7. ✅ Blur/de-emphasize specific dollar amounts
