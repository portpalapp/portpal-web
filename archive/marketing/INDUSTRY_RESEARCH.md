# PORTPAL Industry Intelligence Report: North American Longshore Industry

**Research Date:** February 2026
**Purpose:** Market expansion planning for PORTPAL shift-tracking application

---

## Executive Summary

The North American longshore industry represents a significant addressable market of approximately **140,000+ active union members** across two major unions (ILWU and ILA). With complex pay structures, shift differentials, and automation concerns driving worker frustration, PORTPAL is well-positioned to expand beyond BC Local 500.

**Key Findings:**
- Total addressable market: 140,000+ longshoremen
- At $99/year: TAM = $13.9M+ annually
- ILWU West Coast is the most similar to BC operations (priority target)
- ILA East/Gulf Coast represents the largest single market (85,000 members)
- Pay complexity and grievance tracking are universal pain points

## Part 1: ILWU (International Longshore and Warehouse Union) - West Coast

### Overview
- **Total Membership:** ~42,000 members in 60+ local unions
- **Coast Longshore Division:** 22,000-23,500 dockworkers at 29 West Coast ports
- **Geographic Coverage:** California, Washington, Oregon, Alaska, Hawaii, British Columbia
- **Single Contract:** Pacific Maritime Association (PMA) covers all 29 US seaports

Source: [ILWU Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Longshore_and_Warehouse_Union)

### ILWU Canada (Current PORTPAL Market)
- **Total Membership:** 7,200+ members across 12 locals
- **Local 500 (Vancouver):** ~1,318 union members + 1,422 casuals (2016 data)
- **Contract:** BCMEA collective agreement (separate from US PMA)
- **Median Wage:** $162,000/year (2023 contract)

Source: [ILWU Canada](https://www.ilwu.ca/)

Source: [ILWU Coast Longshore Division](https://ilwulongshore.org/)

### ILWU Pay Structure Complexity

The ILWU has a highly complex pay structure similar to BC, making PORTPAL highly relevant:

**Base Hourly Rates (2024):**
- Starting casuals: $54.85/hour
- Experienced workers: Higher based on classification

**Shift Differentials:**
- Second shift: 1.33x base pay
- Third shift: 1.6x base pay

**Overtime Rules:**
- First/Second shift OT: 1.5x
- Third shift OT: 1.8x

**Average Earnings (2022):**
- Longshore workers (full-time): $197,514/year
- Clerks: $220,042/year
- Foremen/Walking Bosses: $306,291/year

Source: [Bandana Resources](https://resources.bandana.com/resources/how-much-do-pacific-coast-longshoremen-make-real-pay-salary)

### Recent Contract (2023)
- 32% pay increase over 6 years
- Average earnings projected to reach $260,000/year by 2029
- Benefits packages add ~$100,000/year

Source: [Maritime Executive](https://maritime-executive.com/article/ilwu-members-approve-long-awaited-labor-contract-for-west-coast-ports)

## Part 5: Union Structure and Communication

### How Locals Communicate with Members

| Channel | Usage | PORTPAL Opportunity |
|---------|-------|---------------------|
| **Union Meetings** | Monthly/quarterly | In-person demos, word-of-mouth |
| **Newsletters** | 97% of members want them | Feature articles, testimonials |
| **Facebook Groups** | Most popular social platform | Organic sharing, ads |
| **Text Messages** | Dispatch notifications | Integration opportunity |
| **Union Halls** | Daily dispatch | Posters, QR codes |
| **Word of Mouth** | Primary trust channel | Referral programs |

Source: [UnionTrack](https://uniontrack.com/blog/rethinking-union-member-communication-and-engagement)

### Existing Technology in Use

**Dispatch/Management Software:**
- **Harbor Watch** (Dispatch Dynamics): Used by ILA for 7+ years, 5,000+ users
- **LaborPower** (Working Systems): Used by ILWU and others, 500+ union offices
- **Union Impact**: Modern dispatch with mobile notifications
- **Union Worx**: iOS/Android apps for job bidding, dues, messaging

Source: [Dispatch Dynamics](https://dispatchdynamics.com/), [Working Systems](https://workingsystems.com/)

**Key Insight:** These tools focus on **dispatch** and **union management**, NOT on helping individual workers **track their pay** and **verify their paychecks**. PORTPAL fills a gap.

### How Workers Choose Apps/Tools

1. **Union endorsement** - Most trusted source
2. **Co-worker recommendations** - Word of mouth critical
3. **Simplicity** - "Does it solve my problem?"
4. **Privacy** - Union members wary of employer monitoring
5. **Cost** - Must justify value vs. free alternatives

## Part 6: Pain Points by Region

### Universal Pain Points (All Ports)

| Pain Point | Severity | PORTPAL Solution |
|------------|----------|------------------|
| **Complex pay calculations** | HIGH | Automatic rate lookup |
| **Shift differential confusion** | HIGH | Pre-calculated by shift type |
| **Overtime verification** | HIGH | Track OT hours and rates |
| **Paycheck errors** | MEDIUM | Compare expected vs. actual |
| **Grievance documentation** | MEDIUM | Export shift history |
| **Tax planning** | MEDIUM | Annual earnings projections |

### Region-Specific Issues

#### West Coast (ILWU)
- **Automation anxiety** - New contract maintains restrictions, but concern persists
- **Casual worker pathway** - 8,000+ casuals tracking hours toward "A" status
- **Multi-terminal work** - Workers move between facilities
- **High earnings = high stakes** - $200K+ average means errors are costly

#### East Coast (ILA)
- **Recent strike aftermath** - Workers now more aware of pay issues
- **62% raise complexity** - New contract means new calculations
- **Port variation** - 36 ports with some local differences
- **Automation ban** - Less tech-forward culture overall

#### BC Canada (ILWU)
- **Similar complexity to US ILWU** - Differentials, subjobs, location-based rates
- **19.2% raise (2023)** - New rates to learn
- **Smaller market** - 7,200 members, but high adoption potential

### Ports with Most Pay Disputes

Based on news coverage and union activity:

1. **Port of NY/NJ** - Largest volume = most disputes
2. **Houston** - Multiple locals, coordination issues
3. **LA/Long Beach** - Highest volume, complex operations
4. **Savannah** - Rapid growth, workforce scaling issues

### Worker Frustration Indicators

| Indicator | Evidence | Opportunity |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **2024 ILA Strike** | 45,000 workers, 36 ports | Pay is top issue |
| **2023 BC Strike** | 13-day walkout | Contract complexity |
| **Automation protests** | Both coasts | Job security anxiety |
| **High turnover in casuals** | 30%+ annually | Need to track progress |

Sources: [2024 Port Strike Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_United_States_port_strike), [2023 BC Strike Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_British_Columbia_port_strike)

## Part 7: Actionable Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (0-6 months)

1. **Validate ILWU pay structure similarity**
   - Obtain PMA contract documents
   - Map US ILWU differentials to BC structure
   - Confirm app can handle US rates with minimal changes

2. **Identify champions in target locals**
   - Seattle/Tacoma (geographic proximity)
   - LA/Long Beach (largest market)
   - Connect through ILWU Canada relationships

3. **Create US-specific marketing materials**
   - Emphasize "verify your paycheck" value proposition
   - Use US dollar amounts and wage examples
   - Reference 2024 strike and pay concerns

### Medium-term (6-12 months)

4. **Launch pilot in one ILWU local**
   - Recommend: **Local 19 (Seattle)** or **Local 23 (Tacoma)**
   - Leverage geographic and cultural proximity to BC
   - Offer free trial to first 100 users

5. **Build ILA version roadmap**
   - Research ILA master contract pay structure
   - Identify key differences from ILWU
   - Estimate development effort

6. **Establish union relationships**
   - Attend ILWU caucus meetings
   - Connect with local union officers
   - Explore official endorsement path

### Long-term (12-24 months)

7. **Expand to ILA markets**
   - Start with one progressive local (Savannah or Charleston)
   - Build ILA-specific features
   - Target 2027 contract cycle for maximum relevance

8. **Consider enterprise/union licensing**
   - Offer bulk pricing to locals
   - Explore union hall integration
   - Build dispatch system integration
